BIOGRAPHY OF NELSON MANDELA

NELSON ROLIHLAHLA MANDELA, a South African Politician and Philantropist.
Non-violence Anti-Apartheid Activist and A winner of Nobel Peace Prize.

PERSONAL DETAILS

  • Born on 18th July 1918
  • At the village of Mvezo, Eastern Cape
  • His father was Nkosi Mphakanyiswa Gadla Mandela
  • His mother was Nonqaphi Nosekeni
  • Married Walter Sisulu's Cousin, Evelyn Mase in 1944
  • Had two sons, Madiba Thembi and Makgatho
  • Had two daughters both called Makaziwe
  • Married a social worker, Winnie Madikizela on 14 June 1958 during the treason trial
  • Married Graca, his third wife on his 80th Birthday in 1998
  • Died on 5 December 2013

EDUCATION HISTORY

  • Attended Primary School in Qunu
  • Completed Junior Certificate at Clarkebury Boarding Institute
  • Went on to Healdtown, a Wesleyan Secondary School
  • Began Study for Bachelor of Arts degree at University College of Fort Hare which he was unable to complete
  • Completed his BA through the University of South Africa and went back to Fort Hare for graduation in 1943
  • He began studying for an LLB at University of Witwatersrand.
  • Left the school in 1952 without graduating
  • Started studying again through the University of London after imprisonment in 1962
  • In 1989, he otained an LLB through the University of South Africa where he graduated in absential at a ceremony in Cape Town

POLITICAL LIFE AND ACHIEVEMENTS

  • Got Involved in Politics in 1942
  • Joined African National Congress (ANC) in 1944
  • Rose through the ranks of the ANCYL and through its effort, the ANC adopted a more radical mass-based policy, the Programme Action in 1949
  • He was Chosen as the National Volunteer-in-Chief of the Defiance Campaign with Maulvi Cachila as his Deputy
  • Established South Africa's first black law firm, Mandela & Tambo in conjunction with Oliver Tambo.
  • On 10 May 1994, he was inaugurated as South Africa's first democratically elected President.
  • In 1999, Mandela stepped down after one term as President
  • Established Nelson Mandela Foundation and The Rhodes Foundation
  • Mandla Mandela, his grandson was installed as head of the Mvezo Traditional Council at a Ceremony at the Mvezo Great Place.
  • Mandela faced many trials and accusation during protests and struggle before he was able to end White Minority Rule
  • He was released from prison on 12 August 1988
